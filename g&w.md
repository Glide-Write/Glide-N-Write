# Glide & Write (G&W) - Comprehensive Documentation and Wiki

## 1. Purpose of the Project and Origin Story

**Glide & Write** is an innovative communication (AAC - Augmentative and Alternative Communication) application developed to enable non-verbal individuals or those with speech difficulties to communicate quickly and effectively with people around them (doctors, teachers, police, family, etc.).

**Inspiration:** My wife was laughing while watching a stand-up routine by a comedian named Ahren Belisle on YouTube Shorts. Ahren was non-verbal, but he was performing stand-up by typing on his phone and having the text read aloud. I was personally very impressed by this and thought deeply about how someone who cannot speak could communicate more quickly and effectively, whether doing stand-up or in a simpler conversation, for example, with a doctor, teacher, police officer, etc., and developed Glide & Write. At first, it might seem like a difficult method to learn, perhaps like learning a new language, but I believe that once people get used to it and create their own combinations, they can use it very effectively. Even if just 10 people in the world experiencing speech difficulties use this at work, with family, or in a cafe to make their lives easier and more comfortable, this project will have achieved its goal for me.

---

## 2. Basic Operating Principle

At the center of the application is a wide **"Swipe Area"**. Users form words by swiping their fingers in specific directions (UP, DOWN, RIGHT, LEFT) on the screen. Each direction or combination of directions (e.g., UP -> RIGHT) corresponds to a word predetermined by the user.

While looking at the screen, users can visually see (next to the arrows) which word will be produced if they swipe in a particular direction in their current combination.

---

## 3. Input Modes

Two different typing methods are provided based on the users' dexterity and habits:

### 3.1. Step Mode
In this mode, every directional movement and selection is done step by step, with pauses.
*   **How it works:** The user swipes their finger in one direction and lifts it. Then, they swipe in another direction to continue the combination or tap the screen once to select/confirm the word.
*   **Use Case:** It is the ideal mode for beginners to grasp the logic of the system and learn the combinations slowly and confidently without making mistakes.

### 3.2. Glide Mode
This mode reveals the true "fast communication" capability promised by the application.
*   **How it works:** The user glides between directions without ever lifting their finger from the screen. For example, they swipe down, then right without lifting. The moment they lift their finger from the screen, the word represented by that combination is instantly written into the text box.
*   **Repeating the Same Direction:** If the user wants to go in the same direction twice (e.g., RIGHT -> RIGHT), they swipe right, pause for a very short duration (adjustable), and swipe right again.
*   **Use Case:** It allows experienced users who are accustomed to the application to string together an entire sentence in seconds without lifting their finger.

---

## 4. Operating Modes

With the Toggle button in the top right corner, the application's behavior can be switched between two states:

### 4.1. Talk On (Normal Mode)
This is the application's daily usage mode. Swiping gestures add words to the text box.

### 4.2. Entry On (Record and Edit Mode)
This is the mode where the user creates their own personal dictionary and programs the app to suit their needs.
*   **How it works:** When this mode is activated, the screen background turns a distinct red color (to warn the user about the mode change). Swiping in this mode does not type a word; instead, an **"Add/Edit Word"** window pops up on the screen.
*   **Function:** The user determines which word will be assigned to the swipe combination they just made (e.g., LEFT -> UP). This way, everyone can assign their frequently used words to the gestures that make the most logical sense to them.

---

## 5. Layer System

The secret to being able to write tens of thousands of words despite having only 4 main directions is the **Layer** system. With a combination of up to 4 gestures (the sum of 1, 2, 3, and 4-gesture combinations), **340 unique words** can be defined in a single layer. (Combinations of more than 4 gestures can also be made).

A total of 4 different Layers are provided to multiply the user's vocabulary:
*   The user can assign completely different words to each layer.
*   **Changing Layers:** Switching between layers can be done in a second by pressing the physical 1-2-3-4 buttons on the screen or by doing a **two-finger up/down swipe** gesture in the swipe area.
*   This system allows an individual to carry thousands of words in the device's memory and their own muscle memory.

---

## 6. Quick Symbols and Suffix Feature

There are 14 quick access buttons located right under the swipe area.

*   **Features:** These buttons, which can hold a maximum of 5 characters, can be assigned punctuation marks, emojis, or frequently used short words (yes, no, etc.).
*   **Templates:** 4 different templates can be created for this 14-button area from the Settings section. (e.g., One template can be just punctuation, another emojis, another numbers).
*   **Prefix Hyphen (Suffix) Feature:** This is one of the smartest features of the application. If text starting with a hyphen (e.g., `-ing`, `-s`, `-ed`) is added to the swipe combinations or Quick Symbols via "Entry Mode", the application perceives this as a **suffix**. When this suffix is tapped or swiped, it attaches to the end of the previously written word **without a space** (e.g., if you write "play" and then do the "-ing" combination, the word automatically becomes "playing").

---

## 7. Basic Action Buttons

There are 4 main buttons at the top of the interface to manage communication:
1.  **Clear:** Instantly deletes all text in the text box. Used to start a new sentence.
2.  **Undo:** Deletes the last swipe gesture made or the last word written. A lifesaver for erroneous swipes.
3.  **Type (Keyboard):** Opens the operating system's standard keyboard if the user wants to type a word or proper noun that is not defined in their system and is rarely needed.
4.  **Speak (Text-to-Speech):** The most vital button. It reads the text in the text box out loud (to the person opposite) using the device's built-in Text-to-Speech engine.

---

## 8. Configurations

Users don't have to be limited to just one dictionary. The application offers completely independent **Configuration (Profile)** environments.

*   **Different Languages and Environments:** A user can create one configuration as "English - Daily", another as "Turkish", and another as "School/Work Terms".
*   **Voice Engine Integration:** Each configuration has its own "Target Language". This way, when switching to the "English" configuration and pressing the "Speak" button, the device automatically uses an English accent and voice engine.

---

## 9. Settings

A rich settings menu is available for customizing the application:

*   **Appearance (Themes):** 6 carefully selected, easy-on-the-eyes, high-contrast color themes (Bone, Oatmeal, Slate, Sage, Charcoal, Midnight).
*   **UI Language:** Changing the language of the app's menus and buttons (English, Turkish, etc.).
*   **Glide Pause Duration:** Precise adjustment in milliseconds (ms) of the waiting time required to go twice in the same direction when using Glide Mode. This is a critical accessibility setting since everyone's reflex time is different.
*   **Layer Toggle & Settings:** Limiting the number of layers (if 4 layers are too many for the user, it can be reduced to 2) and choosing whether the layer change buttons will stay on the right or left of the screen (Left/Right-handed compatibility).
*   **Quick Symbols Editor:** The area where the content of the 14 buttons within the 4 different templates mentioned above is individually changed and edited.

---

### In Summary;
**Glide & Write** is more than just software; it is a new, fast, customizable, and powerful **"digital language"** platform for individuals with speech impediments. Once it is personally taught to the device (Entry Mode) and muscle memory is established, it has the potential to provide a revolutionary ease of communication in daily life.
